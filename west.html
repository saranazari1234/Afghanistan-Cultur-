West Afghanistan

Historical Background

Western Afghanistan has a deep historical and cultural heritage, particularly influenced by Persian culture and architecture. The city of Herat, often called the “Pearl of Khorasan,” was once a cultural capital of the Islamic world.

Architecture & Arts

The region is known for its Islamic art, intricate tile work, minarets, and ancient monuments. Herat’s Friday Mosque is a stunning example of traditional architecture.

Culture & Lifestyle

The majority of people here speak Dari. Traditional music and poetry, especially ghazals, are central to cultural life. Carpets woven in Herat are famous for their fine quality and beauty.

Main Provinces

Key provinces in the west include Herat, Farah, Ghor, and Badghis.
