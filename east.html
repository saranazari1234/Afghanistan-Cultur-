East Afghanistan

Geography & People

Eastern Afghanistan is a mountainous region with lush green valleys, especially in provinces like Nangarhar, Kunar, and Laghman. The region shares a border with Pakistan and has strong cross-border tribal ties.

Languages & Culture

Pashto is widely spoken here. The culture is deeply rooted in tribal codes, poetry, and oral traditions. Folk stories and moral tales are passed down generations.

Clothing & Food

Men wear traditional shalwar kameez and pakol (round hats), while women’s clothing includes colorful dresses with embroidery. Famous foods include Bolani, Ashak, and local river fish dishes.

Important Sites

The city of Jalalabad is the cultural heart of the region, known for its gardens, poetry festivals, and Pashto literature.
