South Afghanistan

Geography & Ethnic Groups

Southern Afghanistan includes vast deserts and mountain ranges. It's known for its warm climate and traditional tribal systems. The dominant ethnic group in this region is the Pashtuns, with a strong sense of cultural identity and hospitality.

Major Cities

The key city in the south is Kandahar, historically the capital of Afghanistan and a major center of Pashto language, trade, and politics. Other provinces include Helmand, Zabul, and Uruzgan.

Traditions & Celebrations

Southern Afghans are known for their traditional hospitality, unique clothing (such as long turbans and waistcoats), and folk music. Celebrations often include poetry readings, drum beats (dhol), and dances like the Atan.

Clothing & Handicrafts

Pashtun clothing includes loose garments, turbans for men, and colorful dresses with embroidery for women. Handicrafts such as carpets and woven fabrics are also a major part of the culture.
